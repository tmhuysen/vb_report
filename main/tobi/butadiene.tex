\section{Butadiene}
\begin{itemize}
\item $P_z$ Middle carbon = $\phi_1$
\item  $P_z$  Outer neighbour = $\phi_3$
\item  $P_z$  Other middle carbon = $\phi_2$
\item  $P_z$  His neightbour = $\phi_4$
\end{itemize}
All ONVs are also appended by a doubly occupied ONV and RHF based basis $\ket{RHF}$ this is omitted for convenience.
All ONVs use integer $\ket{1}$ instead of $\ket{\phi_1}$. An example of an ONV is $\ket{1_\alpha | 2_\beta}$ left side is always alpha, right side is always beta.
\subsection{Valence bond states}
\subsubsection{Covalent wave function}
\begin{align}
 \psi_{cov} & = ( \ket{1 | 3}   +  \ket{3 | 1})  \bigotimes  (\ket{2 | 4}   +  \ket{4 | 2} ) \\
 & = \ket{12 | 34} + \ket{14|23} + \ket{23|14} + \ket{34|12} 
\end{align}


\subsubsection{Radical wave function}
\begin{align}

 & = \ket{13 | 24} + \ket{14|23} + \ket{23|14} + \ket{24|13} 
\end{align}

\subsection{Full delocalisation}

In this section we simply take all possible determinants in the 4 $P_z$ orbitals with 2 $\alpha$ and 2 $\beta$ electrons. Every valence bond "state" is now a single determinant. There are ${2 \choose 4} $ alpha and beta possibilities yielding a total of 36 determinants. 
\begin{align}
 \psi_{cov} & = ( \ket{1 | 3}   +  \ket{3 | 1})  \bigotimes  (\ket{2 | 4}   +  \ket{4 | 2} ) \\
 & = \ket{12 | 34} + \ket{14|23} + \ket{23|14} + \ket{34|12} 
\end{align}
